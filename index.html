PNHS ICT Lab Preventive Maintenance Q&A

Explore questions and answers about our preventive maintenance proposal

1. Purpose

What specific problem in PNHS are you trying to address with this proposal?

We want to address the issue of slow, malfunctioning, or easily damaged computers in the ICT lab due to lack of regular maintenance.

Why is preventive maintenance important for the school’s computer laboratory?

It keeps the computers in good condition, reduces breakdowns, and ensures students and teachers can use them effectively for learning.

If the computers can still function without preventive maintenance, why do we need it?

Functioning without care will only cause bigger, more expensive problems later. Preventive maintenance saves time and resources in the long run.

How is this different from just repairing computers when they break?

Repair is reactive—it only happens after damage. Preventive maintenance is proactive—it avoids problems before they happen.

2. Relevance

How does this proposal benefit the students directly?

Students will have faster, more reliable computers for activities, projects, and research, leading to better learning experiences.

What will teachers gain from implementing preventive maintenance?

Teachers won’t waste time fixing technical problems during lessons. It helps them teach smoothly using technology.

In what way does this project support the goals of ICT education in PNHS?

ICT education requires functional computers. With preventive maintenance, students gain consistent access to tools they need for skills development.

Why is this project more urgent than other possible proposals for the school?

Computers are used daily and are central in modern education. Without maintenance, the lab may become unusable, directly affecting student performance.

3. Feasibility

Who will be responsible for carrying out the preventive maintenance?

The ICT coordinator, with assistance from trained student volunteers and support from our group.

How often do you plan to conduct the maintenance?

At least once every two months, with minor checks weekly if possible.

What resources or tools will be needed, and are they already available in the school?

Basic tools like screwdrivers, cleaning kits, and software checkups. Some are already available in the ICT room, while the rest are low-cost.

Do you think your group’s plan is realistic for a large school like PNHS?

Yes, because the plan is simple, low-cost, and can be scaled. We focus on one lab first, then expand as needed.

4. Implementation

Can you walk us through the step-by-step process of how this project will be done?

Step 1: Inspection of computers. Step 2: Cleaning and checking for dust or loose parts. Step 3: Software updates and scans. Step 4: Recording results. Step 5: Repeat schedule every 2 months.

What is your proposed schedule for maintenance, and why did you choose it?

Every two months, because this balances between avoiding frequent interruptions and keeping computers in good shape.

How will you monitor if preventive maintenance is actually effective?

By recording the performance of computers before and after maintenance, and tracking reduced breakdown reports.

What role will students or ICT staff play in making this project work?

Students can help in basic cleaning and reporting issues, while ICT staff handles technical parts.

5. Challenges / Sustainability

What possible problems might arise during implementation?

Lack of time, limited cleaning materials, or not enough volunteers.

How will you make sure the project continues even after your group is no longer here?

We propose creating a simple checklist system that ICT staff and future student leaders can continue using.

What if the school does not have enough funds for tools or cleaning materials?

We can use low-cost alternatives, reuse materials, or request small support from PTA or donations.

How will you measure the long-term success of your project?

If computers last longer, break less often, and remain usable for classes, then the project is successful.
